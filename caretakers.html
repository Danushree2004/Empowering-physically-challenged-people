Information for Caretakers

Being a caretaker for a disabled individual can be both rewarding and challenging.
Here are some resources and tips to help:

Tips for Caregiving:

  • Be patient and understanding.
  • Communicate openly and listen actively.
  • Learn about the specific needs and preferences of the person you are caring for.
  • Take care of yourself and seek support when needed.

Here are some organizations and services that provide support for caretakers:

  • Care.com - Online platform for finding caregivers and care jobs.
  • AARP Caregiving Resource Center - Information and support for family caregivers.
  • Caring.com - Resources for caregivers, including articles, community forums, and a directory of senior care providers.

Support Groups:

Joining a support group can provide emotional support and practical advice from others who understand what you are going through.
Here are some options:

  • Family Caregiver Alliance - Offers online support groups and resources for caregivers.
  • Alzheimer's Association - Provides support groups for caregivers of individuals with Alzheimer's and other dementias.
  • Disabled World - Directory of online support groups for caregivers of disabled individuals.
